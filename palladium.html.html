Palladium is a chemical element, symbolized as Pd, and is a rare, silvery-white transition metal. It's known for its high absorption capacity for hydrogen, catalytic properties, and resistance to corrosion. Palladium is often used in catalytic converters, jewelry, dental applications, and hydrogen storage.

Palladium was discovered by William Hyde Wollaston in 1803. Wollaston, an English chemist, isolated palladium from platinum ore. The name "palladium" comes from the asteroid Pallas, which was discovered around the same time. Palladium is a rare and valuable precious metal, often found with other platinum group metals.

Palladium has various applications, including:


Hydrogen storage: Palladium's high hydrogen absorption capacity makes it useful for hydrogen storage and purification.


Dental applications: Palladium is used in dental alloys for crowns, bridges, and dentures.


Jewelry: Palladium is used to make white gold and other jewelry due to its white color and durability.
