Platinum is a dense, malleable, and ductile metal, renowned for its resistance to corrosion and chemical reactivity. Platinum is one of the rarest elements in Earth's crust and is often found in natural deposits alongside other platinum-group metals.

Platinum was first encountered by pre-Columbian civilazations in South America, where it was used to craft small objects mixed with gold. However, Europeans formally discovered platinum in the 18th century, when Antonio de Ulloa, a Spanish scientist and naval officer, documented its existence in 1735 during an expedition to Colombia. The name platinum comes from the Spanish word "platina," meaning "little silver," due to its resemblance to silver.

Platinum has various applications, including:


Jewelry: Valued for its lustrous appearance, durability, and resistance to tarnish, platinum is widely used in high-end jewelry.


Hard disks: Used in magnetic coatings for date storage devices.


Glass Production: Platinum crucibles are used in the production of specialty glass, such as for LCD screens.
