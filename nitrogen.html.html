Nitrogen is a colorless, odorless, and tasteless gas that makes up about 78% of Earth's atmosphere. Nitrogen is essential for life, being a key component of proteins and nucliec acids in all living oraganisms. While abundant in the atmosphere, it is often in a form that is unusable by most plants and animals.

Nitrogen was discovered by Daniel Rutherford in 1772. He isolated it by removing oxygen and carbon dioxide from air, finding that the remaining das wouldn't support combustion or life. The name "nitrogen" comes from the Greek words "nitron" and "genes", meaning "nitre-forming", reflecting its presence in saltpeter(potassium nitrate).

Nitrogen has various applications, including:


Electronics: Nitrogen is used in various stages of electronics manufacturing, including soldering, etching, and as a protective atmosphere.


Food and beverage packaging: Nitrogen helps to displace oxygen, extending the shelf life of food and beverages by preventing spoilage and maintaining freshness.


Oil and gas: Nitrogen is used for pipeline purging, well stimulation, and as a safety measure in potentially explosive environments.
