Nihonium is a synthetic chemical element. It is highly radioactive and extremely unstable element, classified as a transactinide in the p-block and belonging to group 13 of the periodic table. Produced in laboratories via fusion reactions in particle accelerators, nihonium's properties are largely unknown due to its extremely short half-life, with its most stable known isotope lasting only about 10 seconds.

Nihonium has no practical or commercial applications becuase it is an extremely radiactive, synthetic element that decays in mere seconds. Its sole use is in scientific research, where it helps scientists understand the periodic table, study superheavy elements, and advance the fields of nuclear physics and chemistry.
