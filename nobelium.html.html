Nobelium is a synthetic, radioactive actinide element, named after Alfred Nobel, the founder of the Nobel Prize. It was first discovered in 1957 by a team at the Nobel Institute of Physics in Sweden, though their results were later disputed. A Soviet team at Dubna and researchers at Lawrence Berkeley Laboratory in the U.S confirmed its synthesis in the early 1960s by bombarding curium-244 with carbon ions.

Nobelium has no practical applicationsnand is primarily studied for its role in advancing nuclear chemistry and understnading actinide behavior.
