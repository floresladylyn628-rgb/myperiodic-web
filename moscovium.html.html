Moscovium is a synthetic, superheavy element. It was first synthesized in 2003 by a joint team of Russian and American scientists at the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research in Dubna, Russia. Named after Moscow Oblast, it belongs to Group 15 of the periodic table, alongside nitrogen, phosphorus, arsenic, antimony, and bismuth. Moscovium is highly unstable, with isotopes having half-lives of milliseconds to seconds, and its properties are largely theoritically.

Moscovium has no practical applications due to its instability and short half-life. Its primary use if for scientific research purposes.
