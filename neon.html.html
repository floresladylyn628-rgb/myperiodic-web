Neon was discovered in 1898 by Scottish chemist William Ramsay and English chemist Morris Travers. They discovered neon while experimenting with liquefied air and isolating other noble gases, such as krypton and xenon. The name "neon" comes from the Greek word "neos", meaning "new". Ramsay's discovery of neon led to the development of neon signs, which were first introduced in the early 20th century and have since become a popular form of advertising and art.
